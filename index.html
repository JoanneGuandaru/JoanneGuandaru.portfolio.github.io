COVID-19 Data Exploration Project

The COVID-19 Data Analysis Project provides a thorough investigation into global COVID-19 trends, examining infection rates, mortality, and vaccination efforts across various countries and continents. Using real-world data from OWID and leveraging advanced data analytics; SQL, this project focuses on uncovering patterns in infection and death rates relative to population, tracking vaccination distribution, and assessing the healthcare burden reflected in hospitalization and ICU admissions. Key insights into regional differences in pandemic response and healthcare impact offer a clearer understanding of the factors influencing outcomes across diverse regions, providing valuable context for future public health strategies.

Adidas Sales Analysis Project

The Adidas Sales Analysis Project is a comprehensive exploration of Adidas sales data aimed at identifying key insights into regional performance, product popularity, and store effectiveness. Using a dataset from Kaggle and leveraging Power BI, this project showcases dynamic visualizations and detailed metrics to uncover sales trends across different regions and product categories, highlighting top-performing and underperforming products and stores. Seattle Airbnb Project

In the Seattle Airbnb Project, I developed a Tableau dashboard to analyze Seattle’s Airbnb market, aiming to help a client identify the best location to start a new Airbnb business. Using data from the Seattle Airbnb Open Data on Kaggle, I focused on key factors such as competition levels, average price per bedroom, zip code, property type, and seasonal revenue trends to assess profitability across different areas. Through comprehensive data cleaning and analysis, I joined listings and calendar data to examine booking trends, price variations, and occupancy rates throughout the year. This analysis provides actionable insights into high-demand zip codes, optimal property types, and seasonal revenue patterns, guiding investment decisions for maximizing returns in Seattle's competitive Airbnb market.

Bike Sales Analysis Project

In the Bike Sales Project, I used Excel dashboards to analyze bike sales data acquired from Kaggle, examining how various demographic and socio-economic factors influence purchasing patterns. By focusing on key variables such as income, gender, commuter distance, age, number of children, car ownership, and occupation, I aimed to uncover trends and insights that shape bike sales across different regions (Europe, North America, and Pacific). The data was meticulously cleaned to ensure accuracy, including steps like removing duplicates, categorizing age brackets, and resolving missing or erroneous data. Leveraging pivot tables and interactive dashboards, I explored attributes like marital status and education level to offer a comprehensive view of the factors driving bike purchases. This analysis provides valuable insights into the target demographics for bike marketing strategies, with detailed findings on factors such as income and gender impact, commuter distance preferences, and regional trends.
