Bike Sales Analysis

Introduction

In this project, I developed an Excel dashboard to analyze the factors influencing bike purchases. The primary objective was to understand the demographic and socio-economic variables driving bike sales. The analysis focused on multiple dimensions, including income, gender, commuter distance, number of children, vehicles owned, age, and occupation. Filters were added for region (Europe, North America, Pacific), marital status (Married, Single), and education level (Bachelorâ€™s, Graduate, High School, Partial College, Partial High School). The data underwent thorough cleaning, including removing duplicates, creating age brackets, and addressing missing data and errors. Pivot tables were also used to examine various attributes.

Key Findings

Income and Gender Impact on Bike Sales

  • Overall: Men, on average, earn higher incomes ($60,124) than women ($55,774), consistent across all regions. Europe had the largest income gap, with males earning 18% more than females.
  • By Region:
    • North America: Highest average income for both men ($65,455) and women ($64,909), making this region highly lucrative for bike sales.
    • Europe: Lowest income levels for bike buyers, with women earning $39,136 and men $46,269.
    • Pacific: Close income levels between genders, with women earning $62,917 and men $65,385.
  • By Education: Graduate degree holders have the highest average income ($68,049 for men, $61,698 for women), while partial high school education shows the largest income disparity, with men earning $58,000 and women $32,000.

Insight: Higher income correlates positively with bike sales, especially for men, with the largest disparities seen in Europe and among those with lower education levels.

Recommendation: Target high-income earners with premium models in North America and Graduate Degree holders. In Europe, consider affordable options or targeted promotions for women.

Commuter Distance

  • Overall: Buyers commuting 0-1 miles purchase the most bikes (200 bikes), making this group a key target. The fewest sales come from those commuting over 10 miles (46 bikes).
  • By Region:
    • Europe: Short commutes (0-1 miles) dominate bike sales (102 bikes), while over 10 miles is minimal (3 bikes).
    • North America: Prefers medium commutes (2-5 miles, 70 bikes), with a preference for longer distances (5-10 miles) following.
    • Pacific: Mirrors Europe, with short commutes (0-1 miles) accounting for 38 bikes.

Insight: Short commutes (0-1 miles) are most common among bike buyers, particularly in Europe and Pacific regions.

Recommendation: Market bikes for short-distance commutes and, in North America, emphasize options for medium-distance commutes.

Age Impact on Bike Sales

  • Overall: Middle-aged buyers (31-49 years) dominate, purchasing 336 bikes, which represents 68% of sales. Younger (<30) and older (>50) groups lag significantly.
  • By Region:
    • North America: Highest number of middle-aged bike buyers (155 bikes).
    • Pacific & Europe: Higher middle-aged purchases, with lower engagement among other age groups.

Insight: Middle-aged buyers (31-49) are the most likely to purchase bikes.

Recommendation: Focus marketing on the middle-aged demographic, highlighting health-conscious or active features appealing to this group.

Number of Children

  • Overall: Buyers without children purchase the most bikes (142), while those with five children purchase the fewest (18).
  • By Region:
    • Europe: Childless buyers account for 57 bikes.
    • North America: Childless buyers (42 bikes) and those with one child (48 bikes) dominate.
  • Married Buyers: Married individuals with no or one child are the most likely to buy bikes.

Insight: Households without children are more inclined to buy bikes, with larger families purchasing fewer.

Recommendation: Tailor product offerings for single buyers or small families, focusing on convenience and affordability. Market family-focused options to households with one or two children, especially among married buyers.

Number of Cars

  • Overall: Households with one car had the highest bike purchases (152 bikes), followed closely by those with no cars (151 bikes).
  • By Region:
    • Europe: Car-free buyers show the highest interest (76 sales).
    • North America & Pacific: Strong bike purchases among one or two-car households.

Insight: People without cars or with one car are more likely to buy bikes, especially in Europe.

Recommendation: Market bikes as alternative transportation for car-free households or single-car families, particularly in Europe.

Occupation

  • Overall: Professionals lead bike purchases (150 bikes), followed by Skilled Manual workers (115 bikes). Management and Manual workers are the smallest groups.
  • By Region:
    • North America: Highest number of professionals buying bikes (94).
    • Europe: More clerical workers (64) purchase bikes.
    • Pacific: High sales among professionals (36), fewer among manual laborers (11).

Insight: Professionals, particularly in North America, are the largest buyer group.

Recommendation: Target professionals and skilled workers, offering models suited to their lifestyle and commuting needs. In Europe, target clerical workers with budget-friendly commuting solutions.

Dashboards Filtered by Various Factors

Regions

  • Europe: The dashboard illustrates bike sales trends for the Europe region, summarizing insights on income, age, commuter distance, and other factors.
  • North America: The dashboard presents trends specific to North America, highlighting income, age, commuter distances, and key demographics.
  • Pacific: This dashboard focuses on bike sales in the Pacific region, providing a summary of the insights on various attributes.

Marital Status

  • Married: The dashboard explores bike sales among married individuals, with insights on income, age, commuter distance, and other demographics.

Education

  • Graduate Degree: The dashboard highlights sales trends for those with Graduate degrees, focusing on income and other influencing factors.
  • Partial High School: This dashboard focuses on sales trends among individuals with Partial High School education, providing insights on income levels and purchasing behaviors.

Recommendations

  • Target Middle-Aged Buyers: Focus marketing on middle-aged buyers (31-49 years), as they are the majority demographic for bike purchases across all regions.
  • Expand in North America: Capitalize on the high income and strong sales in North America by offering models suited for longer commutes. Consider financing options or discounts for families with multiple children.
  • Leverage Environmental and Car-Free Living Campaigns: Highlight bikes as sustainable alternatives in Europe, where car-free households are prevalent.
  • Occupational-Focused Advertising: Target professionals with premium models for commuting. In Europe, offer budget-friendly options to clerical workers.

Dashboard Summary

The Excel dashboard allows users to explore bike sales by filtering based on region, marital status, and education. It includes pivot tables with clean, well-organized data, providing a clear view of factors influencing bike sales. This data-driven approach enables the business to identify key demographics, tailor marketing efforts, and make informed decisions to drive growth.
